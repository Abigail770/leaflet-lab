The Cost of Living, Quantified

To use the map's interactive features, click the "skip" and "reverse" buttons or drag the slider bar to view temporal changes.

The Cost of Living Index is used to compare living costs between cities or regions. Housing, food, transportation, healthcare, and other essential expenses are taken into account when calculating the index value.

Use the map to explore the Cost of Living Index between 15 major U.S. cities from 2012-2018.

The Cost of Living Index is for this dataset is relative to New York City, which is set at 100. Cities are then compared to this index. For example, in 2018 the COLI for Columbus, Ohio was 69.13, meaning it was over 30% below the New York City's average.


Increases in Cost of Living Index over time can be seen in some cities, while others stagnate or decline. This could be caused by a variety of factors, including location and wealth. Cities near water bodies tend to be more expensive, as can be remote cities that are more diffcult to access and therefore to supply.
